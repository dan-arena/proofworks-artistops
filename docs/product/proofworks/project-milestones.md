# Project Milestones

## Project: ProofWorks

---

# Purpose

This document defines the major operational maturity milestones for the ProofWorks project.

Milestones represent semantically meaningful delivery targets that demonstrate measurable operational and engineering progression.

Milestones intentionally reflect:

- believable operational maturity
- incremental platform evolution
- engineering capability progression
- portfolio significance
- interview explainability

Milestones are not intended to represent arbitrary sprint groupings or time-boxed delivery cycles.

---

# Milestone Philosophy

Each milestone should:

- produce meaningful operational capability
- demonstrate visible engineering progression
- remain explainable as a coherent delivery target
- support future platform maturity
- create believable operational pressure for additional capabilities

Milestones intentionally align to semantic versioning concepts where appropriate.

# Milestone Governance

Milestones are intentionally owned at the Leadership and Product layers.

Milestones should define:

- operational significance
- maturity progression
- capability grouping
- delivery direction

Milestones should NOT define:

- implementation details
- repository decomposition
- task structure
- technical implementation strategy

Those concerns belong to Architecture and Engineering.

---

# Strategic Outcome

The milestone structure exists to ensure ProofWorks evolves through:

- believable operational maturity
- disciplined delivery sequencing
- explainable engineering progression
- governed implementation practices
- realistic operational pressure

rather than through disconnected feature accumulation.
